## Trump's Second Term First 100 Days: Policy Agenda and Tariffs

I found comprehensive information about Trump's second-term agenda during his first 100 days. Here are the key findings:

### Executive Orders and Scale of Action

In his first 100 days, President Trump signed 143 executive orders, the most of any president in this period, along with 42 proclamations and 42 memorandums. Within the first few days, Trump issued more presidential directives than any recent president in the full first 100 days.

### Major Policy Areas

Major topics on which Trump focused included immigration reform, deportations, applying tariffs on other countries, cutting federal spending, reducing the federal workforce, increasing executive authority, and implementing a non-interventionist foreign policy.

### Tariffs Strategy

Tariffs were a cornerstone of Trump's first 100 days agenda:

- On February 1, 2025, Trump signed three executive orders imposing a 25 percent tariff on all goods from Mexico and Canada and a 10 percent tariff on China.

- In the weeks that followed, Trump targeted steel and aluminium as well as auto imports with tariffs of 25 percent. By April, Trump had placed a baseline 10 percent tariff on goods imported from the rest of the world. China received the highest tariff rate at 145 percent.

- President Trump has moved to swiftly implement his trade policy agenda, resulting in effective US tariff levels that are the highest in over 100 years.

### Market Impact

Markets reacted sharply and negatively to Trump's tariff policies as well as to his repeated criticism of Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell. Trump revised his policies in response, one of the few clear indicators of adjustment in response to external criticism.
